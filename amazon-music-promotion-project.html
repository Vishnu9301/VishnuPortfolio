Optimization of Amazon Music Subscription Promotion Spare Claim Codes

Project Title: Optimization of Amazon Music Subscription Promotion Spare Claim Codes

Duration: 2022 - 2023

Executive Summary

This project, conducted between 2022 and 2023, focused on optimizing the management and distribution of Amazon Music Subscription Promotion spare claim codes. The primary objectives were to reduce financial losses from unused codes, enhance operational efficiency, and improve customer experience. By replacing the outdated regional codes with a new global system, the project achieved significant cost savings and improved code utilization by the end of 2023.

Project Overview

Situation:

In early 2022, it was identified that the existing system for managing Amazon Music Subscription Promotion codes, which was divided between the EU and NA regions, was leading to significant financial losses due to underutilization. The old codes were unlimited in count, complicating management and tracking.

Task:

The project involved revoking access to the old codes, acquiring a new set of global claim codes, and implementing a streamlined system to enhance the utilization and effectiveness of these codes. The success of this initiative was measured through a comprehensive review at the end of 2023.

Project Charter

Objectives:

  • Reduce financial losses from unused promotion codes by over 75% by the end of 2023.
  • Improve the utilization rate of promotion codes by 50% year-over-year.
  • Ensure codes are distributed only to eligible customers through a newly developed SOP.
  • Enhance customer experience and satisfaction by reducing error rates in code distribution.

Scope:

  • Revoke old EU and NA codes by mid-2022.
  • Acquire and implement new global claim codes by Q3 2022.
  • Develop and implement a new SOP by Q4 2022.
  • Train the Customer Service (CS) team on the new SOP by Q1 2023.
  • Continuous monitoring and quality control throughout 2023.

Key Deliverables:

  • New global claim codes fully operational by Q3 2022.
  • Comprehensive SOP for code management implemented by Q4 2022.
  • Training materials delivered and CS team trained by Q1 2023.
  • Monitoring and quality control systems in place by Q2 2023.

Stakeholder Analysis

Key Stakeholders:

  • Amazon Music Global Promotion Team: Led the acquisition and global distribution strategy of the new codes.
  • Customer Service Team: Managed code distribution and directly interacted with customers.
  • Quality Assurance Team: Ensured compliance with the new SOP and monitored code utilization.
  • Finance Team: Oversaw the cost-benefit analysis and financial impact assessment throughout 2022 and 2023.

Stakeholder Management:

  • Held regular bi-weekly meetings with the Amazon Music Global Promotion Team to ensure project alignment.
  • Conducted workshops with the CS Team to ensure compliance and understanding of the new SOP by the end of Q1 2023.
  • Established a continuous feedback loop with the Quality Assurance Team to monitor code utilization and compliance throughout 2023.

Project Plan

Revocation of Old Codes:

Analyzed the financial impact of the old codes in early 2022, identifying a $158,024.74 loss due to underutilization. Successfully revoked access to the outdated codes by mid-2022 and communicated the changes to the CS team.

Acquisition of New Codes:

Collaborated with the Amazon Music Global Promotion Team to acquire a new, limited set of global codes aligned with current business needs by Q3 2022.

Development and Implementation of SOP:

Developed a new SOP by Q4 2022, including stringent customer validation and fraud checks before issuing codes. Implemented the SOP and trained the CS team by Q1 2023.

Training and Quality Control:

Conducted comprehensive training sessions for the CS team in Q1 2023. Established quality control mechanisms by Q2 2023 to continuously monitor code utilization and ensure SOP compliance.

Test Launch Findings

Initial Observations:

During the test launch in early 2023, challenges in code validation were identified, leading to slight delays in customer interactions. Feedback from the CS team highlighted areas for SOP improvement, particularly in handling edge cases.

Adjustments Made:

Enhanced the validation process in Q2 2023 to address the identified challenges. Updated the SOP based on feedback and conducted additional training sessions to address gaps.

Results:

Improved customer response times and code utilization during the test phase. Positive feedback from customers regarding the streamlined process.

Retrospective and Impact

Cost Savings:

By the end of 2023, the project resulted in significant cost savings, reducing the value of unused codes from $158,024.74 in 2022 to $34,927.57, leading to net savings of $123,097.17.

Improved Utilization:

Utilization of the new global codes increased by 50%, with 6.62% of the codes used within the first quarter of 2023, compared to lower utilization rates of the old system.

Enhanced Efficiency:

The new SOP ensured that only eligible customers received codes, reducing the risk of fraud and increasing overall efficiency by 30% compared to the previous system.

Customer Experience:

Customer feedback at the end of 2023 indicated a better experience due to faster response times and more reliable code distribution.

ROI:

The initial year ROI in 2023 was approximately 168.29%, with subsequent years expected to yield an ROI of over 1018.16%, demonstrating the long-term financial benefits of the project.

Future Considerations

Scalability:

The system is scalable, with plans to expand as the volume of codes increases in the coming years.

Long-Term System Upgrades:

Potential system upgrades will be considered every few years to maintain efficiency and security.

Compliance and Security:

Ongoing compliance with security and data protection standards will be ensured through periodic audits and updates.
